Fieldwork Overview

Mechanics

  • The land can be restored to different ecosystems.
  • Each ecosystem would need upkeep, until all the conditions are met for it to be self sustaining.
  • Animals and plants grown are part of an ecosystem, and contribute to the conditions.
  • Forest sprites can help with restoration and upkeep, but cannot be directly controlled.
  • Various machines would be connected to a central hub for easy management.

Dynamics

  • Most of the player's time would be spent on planning and setting up the field.
  • The player should learn the needed conditions for different ecosystems, animals and plants.
  • The field starts out degraded but should be clean by the end of the game.
  • Upkeep could involve gathering, watering, trimming ... until it is automated or self-sustaining.
  • Checking on the field should be a quick and simple activity, if the field is set up well.
  • The rules of different plants, animals and ecosystems can be researched.

Aesthetic

  • The player should feel that they are helping nature, not exploiting it.
  • The player should see the progression visually at a glance.
  • Based on permaculture - Cultivate a self-sustaining ecosystem.
  • Integrate scientific agroecological practices, but should be simple to understand and easy to use.
  • Fieldwork should give a mixture of:
    • Relaxing, satisfying, almost mindless actions.
    • More thoughtful planning, but not total control.
  • Solarpunk themes: combining machines and automation with nature.
  • Should allow for the player to meaningfully customise the field.
