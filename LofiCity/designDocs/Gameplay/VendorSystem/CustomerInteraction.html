Customer Interaction

This refers to the conversation between customer and vendor. It takes place after the customer has chosen what to buy.

Mechanics

  • This mainly applies to random NPCs. Named NPCs should use their own dialogue system.
  • The player has a static list of vague conversation actions, such as:
    • Ask about themselves.
    • Complement them.
    • Offer advice.
    • Encourage them.
    • Keep listening.
    • Show concern.
  • The player also has additional actions they can take:
    • Offer a specific item to them that is not displayed.
    • Apply a discount to an item they got or is in the shop.
    • Trade items: NPC offers items they have, player offers something equal.
  • Different actions can change the state of an NPC, and what they buy.
  • The final state of the NPC determines the reputation received.

Dynamics

  • The player would need to chain options for best results.
  • Asking about the customer should hint as to the best way to help them.
  • With time, the player should learn how to treat each customer.
  • There should be multiple ways to change a customer's state.

Aesthetic

  • The player must pay attention to the NPC, and empathise with them.
  • Learning the customers in a district should make the player feel like part of the community.

Examples

  • A customer that is not confident in themselves can be complemented and encouraged.
  • show concern and offer advice/relaxing food to a customer that is overworking themselves.
