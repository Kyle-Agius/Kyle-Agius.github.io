Street Vending Overview

Players can sell the food and artisan goods that they produce by setting up a street cart.

Mechanics

  • Set up a cart, selecting the items to sell.
  • When open, NPCs in the area will check out the cart, forming a queue.
  • The player focuses on one customer at a time.
  • The customer chooses a list of items to buy, as well as quantity.
  • The player then gets a chance to interact with the customer.
  • The customer is then either served instantly, or moved to another queue until the food is ready.
  • The player can either cook the needed food solo, or have a chef helping.
  • The customer interaction and speed of service determines the reputation earned.
  • The UI for each action should be in a separate floating tab, which the player can move.

Dynamics

  • Learn the patterns of the community in the district and when they are most active.
  • Learn what the different Personalities and Interests want and how best to help them.
  • The player is incentivized to treat customers fairly.
  • Having ingredients/dishes ready will reduce wait times.
  • The player will need to shuffle the tabs around to find the needed information.

Aesthetic

  • The player should feel like they are part of the community, and want to help their customers.
  • The player should be rewarded for planning and understanding. Avoid elements of chance.
  • Street vending should feel fast paced and chaotic when compared to fieldwork.
  • Based on principled bargaining:
    • Avoid positional bargaining, i.e. arguing between lower and higher price.
    • Item price is based on an objective measure (market value).
    • Different negotiation options that can lead to mutual gain.
    • Considers the goals of each customer and the player.
